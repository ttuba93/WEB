Chorizo & mozzarella gnocchi bake

  • Time: 35 min
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
olive oil 1 tbsp
onion 1
garlic 2 cloves
chorizo 120 g
tomatoes 800 g
caster sugar 1 tsp
fresh gnochhi 600 g
mozzarella ball 125 g
bazil small bunch
green salad optional

Step 1: Heat the oil in a medium pan over a medium heat. Fry the onion and garlic for 8-10 mins until soft. Add the chorizo and fry for 5 mins more. Tip in the tomatoes and sugar, and season. Bring to a simmer, then add the gnocchi and cook for 8 mins, stirring often, until soft. Heat the grill to high.

Step 2:Stir ¾ of the mozzarella and most of the basil through the gnocchi. Divide the mixture between six ovenproof ramekins, or put in one baking dish. Top with the remaining mozzarella, then grill for 3 mins, or until the cheese is melted and golden. Season, scatter over the remaining basil and serve with green salad.

Easy chocolate fudge cake

  • Time: 55 min
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
sunflower oil 150 ml
self-raising flour 175 g
cocoa powder 2 tbsp
bicarbonate of soda 1 tsp
golden syrup 2 tbsp
large eggs 2
semi-skimmed milk 150 ml
unsalted butter 100 g
icing sugar 225 g
cocoa powder 40 g
milk 2½ tbsp

Step 1:Heat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4. Oil and line the base of two 18cm sandwich tins. Sieve the flour, cocoa powder and bicarbonate of soda into a bowl. Add the caster sugar and mix well.

Step 2:Make a well in the centre and add the golden syrup, eggs, sunflower oil and milk. Beat well with an electric whisk until smooth.

Step 3:Pour the mixture into the two tins and bake for 25-30 mins until risen and firm to the touch. Remove from oven, leave to cool for 10 mins before turning out onto a cooling rack.

Step 4:To make the icing, beat the unsalted butter in a bowl until soft. Gradually sieve and beat in the icing sugar and cocoa powder, then add enough of the milk to make the icing fluffy and spreadable.

Step 5:Sandwich the two cakes together with the butter icing and cover the sides and the top of the cake with more icing.

Easy chicken curry

  • Time: 50 min
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
sunflower oil 2 tbsp
onion 1
garlic 2 cloves
ginger thumb-sized piece
chicken thighs 6
medium spice paste 3 tbsp
tomatoes 400 g
Greek yogurt 100 g
coriander 1 small bunch
ground almonds 50 g
naan breads or cooked basmati rice to serve

Step 1:Heat the oil in a flameproof casserole dish or large frying pan over a medium heat. Add the onion and a generous pinch of salt and fry for 8-10 mins, or until the onion has turned golden brown and sticky. Add the garlic and ginger, cooking for a further minute.

Step 2:Chop the chicken into chunky 3cm pieces, add to the pan and fry for 5 mins before stirring through the spice paste and tomatoes, along with 250ml water. Bring to the boil, lower to a simmer and cook on a gentle heat uncovered for 25-30 mins or until rich and slightly reduced. Stir though the yogurt, coriander and ground almonds, season and serve with warm naan or fluffy basmati rice.
